### Main Points and Ideas
#### Links from the Meeting
* [Google Slides](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1IgLDA_iEVRlz7rZYgifEc1XniojIicyyA95PjQooZD0/edit?slide=id.g3825a1383bc_0_799#slide=id.g3825a1383bc_0_799) -- this was the slideshow that was shared at the beginning of the event.
* [Static Projects to explore](https://lib-static.github.io/models/) -- like the name suggested, this library contains some model of static projects. We were given around 15 minutes to explore these sites on our own, and I was particularly interested in the site "The Letters of Marie Mancini." I liked how simple yet effective this specific site is. However, the library has a lot of sites that I think would be great templates for future projects.
* [CollectionBuilder](https://collectionbuilder.github.io/collectionbuilder-sheets/#how-to-publish-your-google-sheet) -- this was a tool also introduced to me for the first time. I think the tool is for creating a website by uploading metadata, such as those in the form of a CSV. This is my first time learning about a tool like this, and I also realized that our class does work with CSVs sometimes, so maybe this might be helpful.
* [Jekyll Themes](https://jekyll-themes.com/category/academic) -- another website that has templates for websites. I also noticed that the theme we are using for this DAAH portfolio is also made by Jekyll. I think it is really easy to use and customize as well, so I think I might use this for other projects for other classes in the future.

### Reflection
I think it's a really interesting workshop, and I learned a lot from it as well. I actually have classes that requires me to present my final project in the form of a website, so learning about these static sites and the existing templates was super helpful! The hosts of the workshop were also really friendly, I think they were also an NYU alumni. Anyway, I would 100% recommend this workshop, there is a perfect balance between learning about static sites & exploring websites templates on our own.
I also remember asking them if I can publish my GitHub site on a previously bought domain, and they said it is very much possible! The reason why I'm asking is because I'm in the middle of building my own portfolio website, and I wish to have a custom domain for it in the future. But overall, super insightful and fun workshop!
